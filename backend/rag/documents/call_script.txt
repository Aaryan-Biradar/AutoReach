Suggested FFTC outreach call structure:

Opening:
"Hi, this is [name] calling from Food for the Capital. We organize local food drives and community support events in Ottawa. Do you have a quick minute?"

Core ask:
"We're reaching out to see whether your store would be open to supporting a local food drive or donation effort for nearby food banks."

If the manager is interested:
"That's great, thank you. We can send a short summary by email and follow up closer to the event to confirm the details."

If the manager wants written information:
"Absolutely. I'll send over the information and then check back after you've had a chance to review it."

If the manager wants a callback:
"No problem at all. What day or time would work best for a quick follow-up?"

If the manager says they already have a local partner:
"That's great to hear. If there is ever another opportunity or an overflow need, we'd be glad to coordinate around that."

Closing:
"Thanks so much for your time today. We really appreciate your support for local food access in Ottawa."

Tone guidance:
- be brief and respectful
- sound organized, not pushy
- keep the next step simple: email, callback, reminder, or confirmation

FOOD FOR THE CAPITAL — OUTREACH CALL SCRIPT

=== STAGE 1: GATEKEEPER ===

Opening Greeting:
"Hello, I hope you are doing well. I am calling on behalf of Food for the Capital."

Ask for Manager:
"Could I please speak to your manager?"

If asked for context before transferring:
"I'm calling about a local community initiative to see if we might be able to partner with your location for a food drive."

=== STAGE 2: MANAGER PITCH ===

Introduction:
"Hello, my name is Alex and I am one of the executives of Food for the Capital."

The Hook:
"We are a group of local high school students looking to help out in our community by hosting food drives to collect donations for local Ottawa food banks."

Credibility / Impact Statement:
"Our previous events have raised over 17,000 pounds of food and $17,000 for many food banks across Ottawa."

The Core Ask:
"We were wondering if it would be possible to set up a booth in front of your store with 2 volunteers and 1 of our executives present to collect donations. Our events typically run on weekends from 10 AM to 2 PM."

If the store has partnered before:
"We have worked with your stores in our past events and it has benefited both parties greatly and we would love to work with you again."

=== STAGE 3: CLOSING ===

If YES (Store Agrees):
"Thank you so much, we really appreciate your support. Just to confirm, our events typically run from 10 AM to 2 PM on weekends. Could I get your email address so I can send over the formal approval PDF and our promotional poster?"

If NO (Store Policy / Decline):
"I completely understand that corporate policies can be strict. Thank you so much for your time and all the work you do for the community. Have a great day."

If NEEDS MORE INFO:
"I'd be happy to email you our full information package. What is the best email address to send that to?"

If ASKED TO CALL BACK:
"Absolutely, I completely understand. What day and time works best for me to call back?"
