Source: October 2024, March 2025, and January 2026 FFTC tracker spreadsheets provided by the user.

Observed store outreach patterns:
- Some stores accept quickly when asked to review an email summary
- Some stores reject because they are already too tight, already booked, or already running their own food support efforts
- Some stores are interested but want a callback after speaking with another manager or local partner
- Some stores ask for reminders a few days before the event
- Weekend availability matters for some locations

Examples of positive outreach outcomes in the trackers:
- Metro Chapman Mills was marked yes in January 2026
- Independent Grocer Nepean was marked yes in January 2026
- Metro Greenbank was marked yes in January 2026
- FreshCo Strandherd was marked yes in January 2026
- Metro Eagleson was marked yes in January 2026

Examples of cautious or conditional outcomes:
- FreshCo Robertson and Moodie asked for a callback and wanted assurance that support would stay hyper-local to Bells Corners and FAMSAC
- Food Basics Hazeldean needed internal approval after receiving an email
- Real Canadian Superstore Kanata asked for email follow-up and internal review

Examples of common rejection reasons:
- already too tight operationally
- already booked that weekend
- already doing their own food drives

Useful call guidance from the trackers:
- if a manager asks for email, offer a short written summary
- if a manager asks for a callback, capture the day or timing clearly
- if a manager wants support to remain local, acknowledge that and note the neighbourhood or nearby food bank preference
- if a store seems interested but not confirmed, the right next step is usually email plus a reminder call
