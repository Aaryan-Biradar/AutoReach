# FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
## Food for the Capital (FFTC)

---

## WHO IS FOOD FOR THE CAPITAL?

Food for the Capital (FFTC) is a non-profit organization led entirely by high school
students in Ottawa, Ontario. Our mission is to reduce food insecurity in our local
community by organizing food and monetary donation drives at grocery stores across the city.

---

## WHAT DOES FFTC DO?

FFTC organizes weekend food donation drives outside local grocery stores. Volunteers
set up a booth and politely invite customers to donate non-perishable food items or
cash to support Ottawa food banks. Events also raise awareness about food insecurity
in the Ottawa region.

---

## WHO RUNS FFTC?

FFTC is entirely run by high school students. The executive team coordinates with
grocery stores, manages volunteers, and handles all logistics. Trained student
volunteers staff every event under executive supervision.

---

## WHICH FOOD BANKS DOES FFTC SUPPORT?

FFTC partners with several Ottawa food banks including:
- Ottawa Food Bank
- Parkdale Food Centre
- Kanata Food Cupboard
- Barrhaven Food Cupboard
- Stittsville Food Bank
- FAMSAC Food Bank
- Britannia Woods Food Pantry
- Shepherds of Good Hope

All collected food and funds go directly to these partner organizations.

---

## WHERE DOES FFTC OPERATE?

FFTC operates exclusively in Ottawa, Ontario, Canada. Booth events take place
outside grocery stores across the city, including areas such as Merivale,
St. Laurent, Carling, Baseline, Cyrville, Kanata, Barrhaven, and Stittsville.

---

## IS FFTC A REGISTERED ORGANIZATION?

FFTC is a registered non-profit community organization. All funds and food collected
go directly to partner food banks. FFTC operates transparently and has received
recognition from local food banks and community awards programs.

---

## HOW DO STORES BENEFIT FROM PARTNERING WITH FFTC?

Partnering with FFTC demonstrates a store's commitment to the local community.
FFTC booth events are set up entirely outside the store and have zero impact on
store operations. Customers frequently appreciate seeing their grocery store
supporting a meaningful local cause. FFTC can also provide a promotional poster
for the store to display before and during the event.
