FOOD FOR THE CAPITAL — FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Who is Food for the Capital (FFTC)?
Food for the Capital is a non-profit organization led by high school students in Ottawa, Ontario. Our mission is to create a difference within our local community by giving back to those experiencing food insecurity.

What does FFTC do?
FFTC organizes weekend food donation drives outside local grocery stores. We set up a booth with volunteers who politely ask customers if they are willing to donate non-perishable food items or cash to support Ottawa food banks. We also raise awareness about food insecurity in our city.

What is FFTC's impact?
Our previous events have raised over 17,000 pounds of food and $17,000 in monetary donations for multiple food banks across Ottawa. These donations go directly to families and individuals experiencing food insecurity in the Ottawa region.

Who runs FFTC?
FFTC is entirely run by high school students. Our executive team coordinates with grocery stores, manages volunteers, and handles logistics. We have a dedicated group of student volunteers who staff every event.

Which food banks does FFTC support?
We partner with several Ottawa food banks including the Ottawa Food Bank, Parkdale Food Centre, and other local organizations. All donations collected at our booth events go directly to these food banks.

Where does FFTC operate?
We operate exclusively in Ottawa, Ontario, Canada. Our booth events take place outside grocery stores across the city, including locations in Merivale, St. Laurent, Carling, Baseline, and Cyrville areas.

Is FFTC a registered charity?
FFTC is a registered non-profit community organization. We operate transparently and all funds and food collected go directly to partner food banks.

How can stores benefit from partnering with FFTC?
Partnering with FFTC demonstrates a store's commitment to the local community. Our booth events bring positive visibility to the store, and customers appreciate seeing their grocery store supporting a good cause. The setup is entirely outside and non-disruptive to store operations.
