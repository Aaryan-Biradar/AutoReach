# OBJECTION HANDLING GUIDE
## Food for the Capital (FFTC)

---

## OBJECTION: "We don't have space inside the store."

**Response:**
"Oh, no problem at all! We actually set up entirely outside in front of the store, so we
won't take up any floor space or disrupt your checkout lanes. It's just a small table and
a couple of donation boxes right by the entrance."

---

## OBJECTION: "I need to ask corporate / head office first."

**Response:**
"I completely understand. I can call back at a later date to continue this discussion once you’ve had the chance to check with corporate and see what they think. When would be a good time for me to follow up "

---

## OBJECTION: "We already have a donation bin / program."

**Response:**
"That's wonderful that you're already supporting the community! Our booth is a bit different —
our volunteers actually engage with customers to encourage donations, which really boosts
participation. Everything collected stays right here with local Ottawa food banks. Would you
be open to us setting up outside just for one Saturday to see how it goes?"

---

## OBJECTION: "We're too busy right now."

**Response:**
"I totally understand — I know how hectic it can get. The great thing is that the setup is
entirely on us. Your team won't need to do anything at all. We bring our own table, boxes,
and volunteers. We just need permission to set up outside. Would a weekend work better for
your schedule?"

---

## OBJECTION: "There might be liability concerns."

**Response:**
"That's a great question. Our booth is set up entirely outside the store on the sidewalk area,
so it's completely separate from your operations. Our volunteers are trained and supervised by
an FFTC executive at all times. We handle everything from setup to cleanup."

---

## OBJECTION: "We've never done something like this before."

**Response:**
"That's totally fine! Many of the stores we've partnered with were first-timers too, and
they've had a really positive experience. We make it as easy as possible — you just say yes,
and we handle everything else. Would you like me to send you some information about our
past events?"

---

## OBJECTION: "Can you call back another time?"

**Response:**
"Absolutely, I completely understand. What day and time works best for me to reach you?
I want to make sure I'm calling when it's convenient."

---

## OBJECTION: "I'm not the right person to talk to."

**Response:**
"No worries at all. Could you point me to the right person or let me know the best way to
reach them? I'd really appreciate it."

---

## AGENT NOTES
- Lead with empathy before pivoting — acknowledge the concern genuinely before offering a solution.
- If a store mentions keeping donations local to a specific neighbourhood, acknowledge it
  and reference the nearest food bank by name (see Ottawa Food Resources document).
- Never push back aggressively on a hard no — use the graceful decline from the call script.
