# BOOTH EVENT LOGISTICS
## Food for the Capital (FFTC)

---

## OVERVIEW

FFTC sets up a small food collection station outside the front entrance of a
partnering grocery store. The booth collects non-perishable food items and
monetary donations for local Ottawa food banks.

---

## STAFFING

Every booth event is staffed by exactly:
- 2 FFTC volunteers
- 1 FFTC executive (supervises and manages the event)

Volunteers are trained to engage with customers respectfully and share information
about food insecurity in Ottawa. The executive is present for the full duration.

---

## TIMING

- Events run on **weekends (Saturday or Sunday)**
- Standard hours: **10:00 AM to 2:00 PM** (4-hour window)
- This window is chosen to align with peak grocery shopping hours

---

## SETUP AND CLEANUP

- FFTC handles all setup and cleanup — **the store provides nothing**
- Team arrives before 10:00 AM to set up
- All equipment is removed after 2:00 PM
- Store staff are not required to assist in any way

---

## LOCATION

- The booth is set up **entirely outside the store**
- Positioned near the main entrance on the sidewalk or parking lot area
- Does not block any entrances, exits, or fire lanes
- Never inside the store

---

## WHAT IS COLLECTED

- Non-perishable food items (canned goods, dry pasta, rice, cereal, etc.)
- Monetary donations (cash)
- All items and funds go directly to local Ottawa food banks

---

## IMPACT ON STORE OPERATIONS

- **Zero impact on store operations**
- No interference with checkout lanes, aisles, or loading areas
- Customers are free to decline — volunteers never pressure anyone
- The booth is self-contained and self-managed

---

## STORE BENEFITS

- Demonstrates the store's commitment to the local community
- Customers often respond positively to seeing their grocery store support a local cause
- FFTC can provide a **promotional poster** for the store to display before and during the event
- Post-event impact summary available on request (pounds of food collected, monetary total)

---

## INSURANCE AND SAFETY

- The booth operates entirely outside store premises
- FFTC volunteers and executives are always present and supervising
- FFTC complies with local regulations on public solicitation and charitable activities

---

## POST-EVENT

- All collected food is delivered to partner food banks by the FFTC team
- Store can receive a written summary of event results for their records
