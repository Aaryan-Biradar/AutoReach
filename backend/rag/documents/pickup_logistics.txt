FFTC coordinates local food drives and pickup-style donation events with clear advance planning.

From the event organization guide, lead organizers typically work on this timeline:
- 8 weeks before: create the event folder, duplicate the volunteer registration form, and identify target stores
- 6 weeks before: contact stores and divide outreach among team members
- 4 weeks before: contact food banks, confirm the event date, and ask about boxes, posters, and donation pickup
- 3 weeks before: recruit volunteers and organize them by location
- 1 week before: confirm volunteer availability
- 1 day before: send volunteer reminders

Operational notes from the store trackers:
- Some stores ask for an email summary before agreeing
- Some stores prefer a reminder call a few days or one week before the event
- Some stores are open only on weekends for event support
- Some managers want the donation impact to remain local to a nearby food bank or neighbourhood

Volunteer scheduling is typically organized by store location and by day. Past trackers show common event windows such as:
- roughly 11:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.
- roughly 11:45 a.m. to 5:45 p.m.
- roughly 12:00 p.m. to 6:00 p.m.

When discussing logistics on a call, it is safe to say:
- FFTC plans events in advance
- volunteers are organized by store and shift
- the team can follow up by email if the manager prefers written information
- reminder calls or emails closer to the event date are normal

Do not promise exact pickup handling details unless the store has already confirmed them.

FOOD FOR THE CAPITAL — BOOTH EVENT LOGISTICS

What is a food donation booth event?
FFTC sets up a small food collection station outside the front entrance of a grocery store. The booth consists of a table and food bank donation boxes. Our team politely asks customers entering and leaving the store if they would like to donate non-perishable food items or make a cash contribution to support local Ottawa food banks.

Staffing:
Every booth event is staffed by exactly 2 FFTC volunteers and 1 FFTC executive. The executive supervises the event and ensures everything runs smoothly. Volunteers are trained on how to engage with customers respectfully and provide information about food insecurity in Ottawa.

Timing:
Standard events run on weekends (Saturday or Sunday) from 10:00 AM to 2:00 PM. This 4-hour window is chosen to coincide with peak grocery shopping times while keeping the commitment manageable for our student volunteers.

Setup and Cleanup:
FFTC handles all setup and cleanup. We arrive before 10:00 AM, set up our table and donation boxes, and break everything down after 2:00 PM. The store does not need to provide any equipment, supplies, or staff assistance.

Location:
The booth is set up entirely outside the store, typically near the main entrance on the sidewalk or in the parking lot area. We do not set up inside the store, and we do not block any entrances, exits, or fire lanes.

What we collect:
We accept non-perishable food items (canned goods, dry pasta, rice, cereal, etc.) and monetary donations. All collected items and funds go directly to local Ottawa food banks.

Impact on store operations:
Our booth has zero impact on store operations. We do not interfere with checkout lanes, aisles, or loading areas. Customers are free to decline participation — our volunteers never pressure anyone.

Store benefits:
Hosting an FFTC booth demonstrates the store's commitment to the local community. Many customers appreciate seeing their grocery store support a good cause. We can provide the store with a promotional poster to display if desired.

Insurance and safety:
The booth operates entirely outside the store premises. FFTC volunteers and executives are always present to supervise. We follow all local regulations regarding public solicitation and charitable activities.

Post-event:
After the event, FFTC delivers all collected food to our partner food banks. We can provide the store with a summary of the event's impact (pounds of food collected, monetary donations raised) for their records.
